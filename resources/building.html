This video by Raymond(9181C) and Jeremy(9181D) focuses on build habits in VEX.

Key Takeaways

  • Use Aluminum C-Channel for a lighter, quicker robot.
  • Use Steel C-Channel for a heavier, sturdier robot.
  • Avoid cutting C-Channel, as you can't glue it back together. If you are, Have a clear plan of what, where and why you are cutting metal.
  • Make sure that you mount the C-Channel correctly before cutting it.
  • File sharp edges.
  • Use Nylocks not Kept nuts on your final build.
  • Avoid protruding axles.
  • Lock axles with Shaft collars. Make sure these are tight on the flat side of the axle.
  • Use Bearings whenever mounting an axle.
  • Use a variety of different sized spacers to minimize total spacers used.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help!

    Key Takeaways

  • A lift is used to manipulate game objects by moving the object up.
  • A Four Bar Lift is great for new teams, as it is not complex and relatively easy to build.
  • Since the four bar has to reach outwards, it does not have the highest reach vertically.
  • A double reverse four bar is basically two four bars, allowing for extremely high reach.
  • The double reverse four bar is also heavy, which means a higher center of gravity. This creates the perfect scenario for tipping.
  • A double reverse four bar is also relatively hard to build.
  • The scissor lift is great for lifting objects vertically, as it is more balanced than a double reverse four bar.
  • The scissor lift is also extremely hard to build well, as a poorly built scissor lift can result in bending metal.
  • Scissor lifts can also be great for wall-bats, which are robots that block other teams from scoring by physically creating a barrier.
