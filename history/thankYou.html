Thank You...

... for making this program great :)

Mr. Hamade

Former Robotics Teacher and Mentor at Seaquam Secondary

Mr. Hamade has provided extensive support to the Seaquam Robotics Program. Ever since the formation of Seaquam Robotics as the Seaquam Vex Competition Robotics Club, Mr. Hamade was there. He stayed after school for countless hours, helped new robotics students adjust to the program and even taught the Robotics course for robotics students. Through this substantial aid, the Seaquam Robotics program blossomed into the prosperous program it is today, and for that we thank you Mr. Hamade.

Mr. Singbeil

Current Robotics Teacher and Mentor at Seaquam Secondary

Filling in the shoes of our previous Robotics teacher, Mr. Singbeil was added to our program two years ago. In that time, he has showed his dedication to the program. Whether we need help in our building or design, we know that Mr. Singbeil is there to help us through the entire process. It is incredible how he has handled the growth of the program and still managed to give everyone as much shop time as before to work on their robots. Thank you for your dedication to our program and the time you've spent making it great.

The Delta School District

Our Sponsors

The Delta School District has provided our program with the funding and space we require to be competitive. They have also allowed our program to be taken as a course in Seaquam, which allows students to focus on building competitive robots. Just a few years ago, this program only consisted of about twenty or so students in one class. From the support of the Delta School District, we were able to double this figure. For your extended support and faith in our program, we thank the Delta School District.

Mr. Schellenberg

Seaquam Robotics Supervisor

Mr. Schellenberg has been with the Seaquam Robotics Program for many years. He diligently supervises students, while also aiding them in any design problems or questions that may arise thorough the process. His time at the shop has allowed the program to create more complex, competitive robots. For your time investment and aid in the shop, we thank you Mr. Schellenberg.

Owen Espezel

Seaquam Robotics Supervisor

As a former student himself, Owen knew exactly what it took to be competitive in Robotics. He volunteered a great deal of his time passing on this knowledge to the new Robotics students. He was also great at giving them advice with any of the machinery. His support aided teams at every competitive level and his knowledge about build quality rivalled even the best builders at Seaquam. This knowledge and skill combined with your dedication helped all teams in Seaquam, and for that we thank you.
